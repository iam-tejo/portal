Our Leaver Process (Automated & Ad-hoc)

Securing access revocation through automated offboarding and emergency immediate suspensions.

Standard Leaver: End Date Timeline
Daily HR feed (iTrent) triggers end date. AD accountExpires blocks login at midnight T-0. On T+1, Saviynt disables accounts and moves them to Leavers OU. On T+30, accounts are permanently deleted.
AD accountExpires Fail-Safe
By pre-setting AD accountExpires on start, lockouts occur on the end date even if Saviynt is down or unreachable.
Emergency Immediate Suspension
High-privileged admins/managers can suspend accounts instantly, killing active Entra sessions and moving AD accounts to the Suspended OU.

⚠️ Limitations of this Flow

  • Detective, Not Preventative: ServiceNow lacks application database visibility to audit toxic access combinations before request submission.
  • Late-Stage Discovery: SOD violations are flagged only after they reach Saviynt approvals, delaying reviews and creating administrative overhead.

💡 Why Saviynt ARS is Better

Using Saviynt ARS as the front door lets the system block toxic combinations during the request stage itself. It warns the requester and blocks invalid submissions instantly, keeping target systems clean out-of-the-box.

Segregation of Duties (SOD)

By retaining ServiceNow as our request front-door, we lose real-time preventative blocking at the request stage, shifting SOD checks and manual mitigations into the Saviynt approval workflow.

ServiceNow Front-Door Limitation

Because requests originate in ServiceNow, they bypass Saviynt ARS's native front-door checks. We lose the ability to block toxic requests in real time during the request stage itself before submission.

Shift to Approver Mitigation

Since violations cannot be blocked at the front door, they must be resolved during the Saviynt approval workflow. Approvers are forced to manually review risk warnings and select appropriate mitigation controls.

Out-of-Band (OOB) Access Control

Out-of-Band access occurs when administrators bypass governance portals and assign privileges directly in target systems.

Step 1: Scan & Reconcile

Regular delta scans query directories (e.g., AD, ServiceNow) and compare real configurations against Saviynt's baseline.

Step 2: Automated Actions

Saviynt can immediately strip access or trigger retroactive approvals to bring access back into compliance.

Campaign Bucket Templates

We classify applications into standardized campaign templates using acronyms to automate reviews based on risk, schedule, and ownership.

🏷️ Acronym Naming Convention

1. Type:
S = Standard
P = Privileged
2. Cycle:
Q = Quarterly
BA = Bi-Annual
A = Annual
3. Owner:
LM = Line Mgr
EO = Ent. Owner
AO = App Owner
4. Target:
C = Connected
D = Disconnected

🛡️ Campaign & Escalation Rules

  • Self-Certification Prevented: Self-recertifications are blocked and automatically escalate to a Line Manager (LM) campaign.
  • Post-Mover Campaigns: Transfers immediately trigger a targeted Line Manager campaign to prune legacy access.
  • Reminder Notifications: Automated emails are sent periodically to nudge reviewers with outstanding tasks.
  • Fail-Secure Expiration: Access is automatically revoked if the campaign expires before a review decision is made.
